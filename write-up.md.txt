# Design Write-up — Daily Reflection Tree

## 1. Problem Understanding

The assignment focuses on building a **deterministic reflection system** that helps employees evaluate their day through structured thinking.

Unlike AI-based tools, this system:

* Does not generate responses dynamically
* Does not rely on interpretation
* Instead encodes logic into a fixed structure

This shifts the problem from "answer generation" to **knowledge design**.

---

## 2. Design Approach

The system is designed as a **decision tree**, where:

* Each node represents a cognitive step
* Each answer leads to a predefined branch
* The entire flow is predictable and traceable

This ensures:

* Consistency across sessions
* No hallucination risk
* Clear reasoning paths

---

## 3. Axis-Based Reflection Model

The reflection is structured across three psychological axes:

---

### Axis 1: Locus of Control (Victim ↔ Victor)

Focus: Identifying whether users see themselves as passive or active in outcomes.

Design:

* Questions focus on reactions to challenges
* Options reflect real internal thought patterns

Goal:
To help users recognize where they had control without assigning blame.

---

### Axis 2: Orientation (Entitlement ↔ Contribution)

Focus: Shifting perspective from “what I received” to “what I gave”.

Design:

* Questions highlight effort, support, and expectations
* Options subtly capture entitlement without being obvious

Challenge:
Entitlement is often unconscious, so indirect questioning was necessary.

---

### Axis 3: Radius of Concern (Self ↔ Others)

Focus: Expanding perspective beyond self.

Design:

* Questions move from self → team → individuals → end users
* Encourages perspective-taking naturally

Insight:
This axis builds on the previous two, creating a progression.

---

## 4. Question Design

The questions were designed with the following principles:

* **Realistic** → Reflect actual thought patterns
* **Non-leading** → No “correct” answers
* **Clear** → Easy to choose without confusion
* **Reflective** → Encourages pause and thinking

Example:
Instead of asking directly about responsibility, the question focuses on how the user reacted.

---

## 5. Branching and State

Each answer contributes to a **state model**:

* axis1 → internal vs external
* axis2 → contribution vs entitlement
* axis3 → self vs others

These are tracked using signals and used to:

* Route reflections
* Generate the summary

The logic is simple, transparent, and deterministic.

---

## 6. Reflection Design

Reflections are written to:

* Avoid judgment
* Validate experience
* Introduce alternate perspective

Tone:

* Neutral
* Observational
* Slightly forward-looking

---

## 7. Deterministic Design vs AI

A key constraint was avoiding LLM usage at runtime.

Advantages:

* Predictable outputs
* No hallucination
* Easy to audit

AI was used only during development for:

* Generating ideas
* Testing scenarios
* Refining language

---

## 8. Trade-offs

* Fixed options reduce flexibility but improve clarity
* Simple signals reduce depth but increase transparency
* Deterministic flow limits adaptability but ensures reliability

---

## 9. Improvements

With more time:

* Add longitudinal tracking across days
* Introduce adaptive questioning depth
* Expand behavioral scenarios
* Build visualization layer

---

## 10. Conclusion

This project demonstrates how structured thinking can be encoded into a deterministic system.

The key challenge was not coding, but:

* Designing meaningful questions
* Structuring cognitive flow
* Balancing guidance with neutrality

This reflects the core idea of knowledge engineering:
**turning human insight into usable systems.**
